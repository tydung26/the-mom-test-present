# Brainstorm: Mom Test Presentation Redesign

## Problem Statement
Current 19-slide presentation uses a cookbook/iPad example that doesn't resonate. Need a more detailed, universally relatable presentation focused on startup idea validation + getting real value from meetings/conferences. 30-min talk for a mixed tech crowd.

## Decisions Made
- **Example**: Flower shop (replaces cookbook/iPad throughout)
- **Segmentation example**: "Weddings or Weekends?" (replaces Babies or Bodybuilders)
- **Audience**: Mixed tech crowd
- **Duration**: 30 min (~24 slides)
- **Meetings section**: Both casual encounters AND formal VFWPA framework

---

## Replacement Examples

### FAIL Conversation (Slide 4)
> **You:** "I'm thinking of opening a flower shop with unique bouquets and same-day delivery. Would you buy from it?"
> **Friend:** "Oh wow, that sounds lovely! I'd definitely order flowers from you."
> **You:** "We'd do custom arrangements and a subscription service too."
> **Friend:** "I love that! My apartment always needs flowers. You should totally do it."
> *(Compliments + hypothetical future promises. Zero real data.)*

### PASS Conversation (Slide 5)
> **You:** "Those flowers on your table are nice — where'd you get them?"
> **Friend:** "Oh, the market on Sunday. They were like $5."
> **You:** "Do you buy flowers often?"
> **Friend:** "Not really. Maybe for someone's birthday or if I see cheap ones."
> **You:** "When's the last time you actually ordered flowers from a shop?"
> **Friend:** "Hmm... Valentine's Day? For my girlfriend. It was like $60 and they died in 3 days. Kinda felt ripped off."
> **You:** "Did you look at other options?"
> **Friend:** "I just Googled 'flower delivery near me' and picked the first one with good reviews. Didn't really think about it much."
> *(Now you know: casual buyers go to the market for cheap. Gift buyers Google last-minute and care about price-to-value. "Died in 3 days" is the real pain. Subscription idea? They buy flowers maybe 3x a year.)*

### Segmentation Example — "Weddings or Weekends?" (Slide 21)
Replaces "Babies or Bodybuilders?" superfood example.

A flower shop trying to serve everyone — wedding planners want custom + bulk, Valentine's last-minuters want fast + cheap, offices want weekly subscriptions, funeral homes want somber arrangements. Making one happy disappoints the others. Serving everyone = serving no one.

Focus down → **brides-to-be who want a personal florist experience**. You know where to find them (wedding fairs, bridal groups, Pinterest), what they'll pay (premium), and what they actually care about (personalization, reliability, aesthetic).

## Implementation Considerations
- Flower shop example threads through ~6 slides — ensures narrative cohesion
- Chat bubble components already exist — reuse for new FAIL/PASS conversations
- VFWPA slide needs new layout (email template format)
- Conferences slide needs new visual (could reuse snowball circles with different labels)
- Product vs Market Risk needs new visual (two-column comparison)
- Customer Slicing could use a funnel/tree diagram

## Success Criteria
- All 24 slides fit within 30 min talk
- Flower shop example is consistently threaded throughout
- New slides (Fluff, Ideas, Conferences, VFWPA, Risk, Slicing) add depth without dragging
- Meetings/conferences section covers both casual + formal
- Cheat sheet updated to reflect expanded content

## Risks
- 24 slides in 30 min = ~75 seconds per slide — tight. Some slides may need to be quick "beats"
- Adding 5 slides while removing 0 content-heavy slides could make the talk rush
- Mitigation: merged slides (13) + some new slides are simple visual beats (15, 19)
